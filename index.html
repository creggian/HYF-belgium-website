HackYourFuture Belgium

THE CODING SCHOOL FOR REFUGEES IN BELGIUM

What Is HackYourFuture?

Refugees often experience challenges in finding a suitable job to apply and further develop their skills. Yet, the number of unfilled vacancies in the IT sector is increasing at fast pace.

HackYourFuture was founded in 2015 in Amsterdam, with the aim to enable refugees to build digital skills for a career in web development, facilitate the integration of newcomers, and address the shortage of qualified workforce in the IT sector. Since 2015, HackYourFuture coding schools have also opened in Copenhagen and Malmö.

On 20th of May 2018, HackYourFuture Belgium will launch the coding school for refugees at BeCentral, the digital campus in Brussels.

Our Mission

Empower refugees by teaching them the necessary skills for a career in software development.

Our Programme

Our 6 months course is divided into 8 modules of 3 weeks each, with the exception of the final project which takes 6 weeks.

Our Training

Every Sunday expert coaches and students meet at BeCentral Brussels for in-person classes. During the week, students are supported online with their homework.

WHAT DO WE TEACH?

Our six months course is divided into eight modules which cover programming languages such as JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Node, React, and Databasing in MySQL/MongoDB. However, what we value most is that our students learn how to think as a programmer, how to solve problems, and how to manage a project. This fundamental knowledge prepares our students for their first job as a web-developer.

Who can apply?

We teach talented refugees who want to start their career in web development. The course is open to anyone staying in Belgium.

You have to be very motivated to start a career in programming.

You have to speak English at an intermediate level.

You have to study around 25 hours per week to keep up with our program.

We accept students from all over Belgium and we cover transportation costs.

We provide laptops for students that don't have their own during the training.
